Project 1

The webapp is an online store that specializes in selling pet fish and aquariums. It has a user-friendly interface that allows customers to easily browse through different categories of fish and aquariums. Customers can search for different types of fish and aquariums based on their preferences such as size, color, type of fish, type of aquarium, and more. The webapp also provides detailed information about each type of fish and aquariums, including their care instructions, feeding requirements, and compatibility with other fish species. Customers can read reviews from other customers to help them make informed decisions before making a purchase. The webapp also offers a variety of accessories such as fish food, filters, pumps, and other necessary equipment to keep the aquariums in top condition. Customers can add products to their cart and proceed to checkout, where they can make a secure payment using a variety of payment options. Once the order is placed, customers can track their order and receive updates on the status of their shipment. The webapp also provides customer support in case of any issues or questions that customers may have. Overall, this webapp provides a convenient and easy way for people to purchase pet fish and aquariums from the comfort of their own homes.

Project 2

A simple web app that has a database of trees grown in different parts of Africa could have the following features: Home page: A homepage with a search bar that allows users to search for trees by name, location, or other criteria. Tree database: A database of trees with information such as common name, scientific name, description, image, location, habitat, and growth requirements. Tree details page: A dedicated page for each tree that displays detailed information about the tree, such as its height, lifespan, flowering season, and fruiting season. Map view: An interactive map view that shows the locations of trees in different parts of Africa. User profiles: User profiles that allow users to save their favorite trees, create their own lists of trees, and contribute to the database by submitting new tree information. Admin panel: An admin panel for managing the database, approving user submissions, and updating the website content. Mobile responsiveness: A mobile-responsive design that allows users to access the website on their smartphones and tablets. The app could be useful for botanists, researchers, conservationists, and anyone interested in learning more about the trees of Africa.
